The calculator implements the documented business rules below plus the PEFC (Public Education Funding Commission) Opportunity and Operational weights. Opportunity and Operational funding estimates use FY2025–26 statewide funding pools ($163,000,000 and $279,026,800) and base rates calculated from those pools at full internal precision, displayed to the nearest cent. Salary amounts in the cost roll-up remain placeholders until official §1305/§1308/§1311 salary schedules are loaded — they are not used anywhere in the Opportunity or Operational funding math. This is not an official DDOE calculator; treat any line still marked "pending," "confirmed — citation pending," or "discrepancy" in the register as a working estimate, not a settled number.

LEA setup

Assistant Superintendent positions are zero for Charter LEAs (business rule confirmed, districts only). Superintendent, Administrative Assistant, Buildings & Grounds Supervisor, Food Services Supervisor, and Reading Cadre are calculated the same way for both District and Charter LEAs per the confirmed rules — see the Register tab for the open Reading Cadre discrepancy against its cited appropriation.

Schools

Enter one card per school building. Every school calculates separately; all school results roll up automatically into the LEA totals below. Enter each student once, in their single applicable Base-Model category (Pre-K, K–3, Grades 4–12, Basic, Intensive, or Complex Special Education) to avoid unintended double counting when generating Division I units. Basic, Intensive, and Complex counts are shared between the Base-Model unit calculation and the Operational funding weight below — enter them once per school, not twice. Negative or non-numeric entries are treated as zero; results never display as NaN or Infinity.

Plan forward, based on the meeting

  1. Close the "no status" gap before trusting outputs. Several rules in the register have no confirmed legal citation or have "citation pending" status — meaning nobody has signed off that the implemented formula matches the statute. Get each one marked verified/confirmed/rejected by a named business owner before the calculator ships numbers anyone relies on.
  2. Resolve BR-CO-009 (Reading Cadre) first — it's the one with a live dollar impact. The confirmed business rule (1 position per District or Charter) directly conflicts with the cited FY2026 appropriation, which excludes charters and prices the position very differently from the flat rate historically used. Get DDOE's confirmation on the charter-eligibility question and the §1707 equalization value before anything downstream uses this line.
  3. Fill in the two empty categories. "Instructional Supports" and "Administrative Support Professionals" are listed as headers only — no formula, no citation, no business rule. Several of the likely items (Math Coaches, Early Literacy Coaches, Related Services, Opportunity Funding) may be appropriation-funded line items rather than Title 14 unit-generated positions, so they may not fit the unit-based calculation pattern used elsewhere on this sheet.
  4. Load the missing salary schedules. Every compensation-type rule (principal, AP, superintendent, nurse, custodian, secretary) references §1305 / §1308 / §1311 dollar tables that aren't in this workbook. Without them the calculator can generate FTE counts and PEFC dollar estimates, but not position-level salary costs.
  5. Reconcile the Rule Register, the New Rule Updates sheet, and this calculator into one source. As of this update they contain overlapping but not identical information (different schemas, some inconsistent citation/status wording for the same rule). Before the next round of changes, pick one structured source of truth and generate the others from it.
  6. Decide who owns updating the workbook itself. Until there's a named business owner for interpreting/approving each rule, every fix to this calculator is provisional.
